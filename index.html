“Excellence is the gradual result of always striving to do better.”


Established in 1964 as the School of Paper Technology, the Saharanpur campus of IIT Roorkee has come a long way in the fifty years of its existence and has succeeded in establishing its identity as an institution of excellence. This has been made possible by the relentless effort of every individual who has been a part of this campus for the last fifty years. Pravaah is a celebration which commemorates these fifty years and honours every big and small effort that has been put in making this institution progress, develop and reach greater heights.

Pravaah would comprise a myriad of events spread over one year. These would include technical events like workshops and guest lectures from eminent people in the industry, an alumni meet that would bring back the years for the distinguished alumni of this campus, a socio-cultural event and international conferences to boost research and industry-institute relations. Such events would generate great fervour, inculcate a spirit of celebration within the campus populace as well as provide the students an invaluable opportunity to interact with some of the most established individuals in the technical field.

The Saharanpur Campus, which has now become an integral part of Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee, started out as the first institution in India to impart higher level education in Paper Technology. It was established as the School of Paper Technology, in the year 1964, with a tripartite agreement between Government of India, the Government of Uttar Pradesh and the Royal Swedish Government. While the Government of Uttar Pradesh and India provided funds for the allocation of the land and the construction of buildings respectively, Swedish Government, on the other hand, contributed to the providing of laboratory, equipment and technical expertise for starting the institute.

The institute started with a three year Post Metric (Certificate) and two year post B.Sc. (Diploma) courses in Paper Technology with the objective of providing supervisor level 1 and management level manpower respectively, required by the upcoming and expanding paper industry in India. With the objective of achieving high academic standards, active faculty exchange programmes were undertaken with a Swedish institute in Markeryd, Sweden, that was also engaged in the impartation of similar kind of education.

Its progress was quick, right from its early days. In 1968 the School of Paper Technology was renamed as the Institute of Paper Technology, a name in line with of other names of the similar in country.

With development in technology and the growing need for trained personnel, with the boom in the industrial sector in our country, a need was felt for introduction of a course in process instrumentation, which was introduced as a Post B.Sc.(Diploma) program in 1977, marking the milestone for the expansion of the Institute, beyond its beginnings in the Paper Technology.

The growing prosperity and intellect of the institute was witnessed all around, and the institute was merged with University of Roorkee in 1978 as its second campus. A new programme, B.E. (Pulp and Paper) was started and ongoing Diploma courses were renamed as University Diplomas. Gradually, the PhD Programs were introduced. The University of Roorkee recognised University Diplomas as equivalent to M.Sc. Degree. Finally, the Certificate courses were put an end to in the year 1982, as the BE and PhD programs gained momentum. The year 1989 saw an introduction of the ME course in Paper Technology.

In the year 2001, the University of Roorkee, was converted into Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee(IITR). Simultaneously, the Institute of Paper Technology was renamed as the Saharanpur campus of IIT Roorkee with Department of paper technology as one of the academic departments of IIT Roorkee. The University Diploma courses were henceforth discontinued.

An academic restructuring took place in 2004 with the objective of developing Saharanpur campus as multidepartment campus with increased student and faculty strength and hence, infrastructural facilities. As an outcome of this restructuring, two new programmes namely- (i) Integrated M.Tech. Polymer Science and Technology (ii) Integrated Dual degree in B.Tech. Process Engineering with MBA.

With the development of the Polymer industry both abroad and in India, the institute stuck with its tradition of being one of the pioneers in the introduction of a course in a new discipline and started an integrated Master of Technology course in the year 2006 in Polymer Science and Technology. The IDD B.Tech in Process Engineering with MBA course was enhanced to keep up with the demands of the corporate sector, being integrated with a Masters in Business Administration (Integrated Dual Degree) in the year 2007.

In 2013, the Department of Paper Technology was trifurcated, into the Department of Paper Technology, the Department of Polymer and Process Engineering, and the Department of Applied Sciences & Engineering.

FURTHER CONTRIBUTION

  • Throughout its evolution from the School of Paper Technology to the present campus with multiple academic departments, the Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee has made significant contributions in the Technological realm.
  • The Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee played an important role in the formation of The Central Pulp & Paper Research Institute (CPPRI), Saharanpur. The United Nations’ Development Program assigned two R&D projects – one for the study of potential of Indian hardwoods for mechanical pulping and another for chemical pulping. The mechanical pulping pilot plant was setup at the Cellulose & Paper division of the Forest Research Institute (FRI), Dehradun and the chemical pulping project was taken up at Saharanpur taking advantage of the close proximity of the then Institute of Paper Technology. To foster collaboration between these two projects, an exclusive research institute (CPPRI) was set up in Saharanpur adjacent to the Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee.
  • Now, the Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee enjoys a unique location having a national R&D institute in the field of pulp and paper (CPPRI) on one side and a large integrated pulp and paper mill on the other side of the campus. The students of paper technology derive immense benefit from the Star Paper Mills through in plant training and various inputs in their project works. IIT Roorkee and CPPRI have entered into a memorandum of understanding (MOU) for sharing their technical expertise and laboratory facilities.
  • Another outcome of this unique location is that a number of manufacturers of paper mill plant & machinery and laboratory equipment have come up in and around Saharanpur.
  • The Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee has played active role in important policy decisions for the development of pulp and paper industry in India.
  • The Saharanpur Campus of IIT Roorkee has worked in close cooperation with the Indian paper industry. For many years, an advisory committee consisting of top ranking officers of the Indian paper industry provided valuable inputs in conducting academic and research programmes at the campus.
  • The institute has had considerable international influence as well, assisting in setting up training and exchange programmes for students from other countries such as Thailand and Tanzania, helping them progress in its perennial tide of excellence.

ICEMA14
On the occasion of Golden Jubilee of IIT Roorkee Saharanpur Campus, an International Conference on Emerging Materials and Applications “ICEMA 2014” will be organized on April 5-6th, 2014. This conference envisages to bring together researchers, developers, and users from around the world in industry and academia for sharing their views and endeavours to compile an overview of the upcoming trends on the above aspects of emerging materials.

SoCProS13
Apart from that, an International Conference of Soft Computing for Problem Solving “SocProS13” will be held on December 26-28, 2013 at the Noida Campus. This conference is intended to assemble researchers, developers, and users from the Soft Computing fields to provide an extract on the significance of computing in the core sector areas of varying fields.

In order to boost industry institute interaction and provide the students with a dynamic platform to attain valuable interaction with the established personalities in the technical field, we plan to conduct a series of guest lectures that would provide insight and expert analysis on a myriad of topics ranging from the scope and opportunities in the core industry to research openings in different areas.

Lamhe, the alumni meet, as aptly called, will be the highlight of the Golden Jubilee Celebrations taking place in the academic year 2013-14 where the birds which left this campus long back shall be invited to breath in, a lungful of the same air, that nourished them. This is a great opportunity for the present students of this campus where they shall be exposed to an imperative platform of interaction with the alumni.

The industry institute meet would involve interactive sessions and panel discussions with the pioneers in the respective industrial sector. The primary focus from the institute’s side would be developing relations and improving networking with the bigwigs of the core sector while the people from the industry would also be interested in gauging future personnel recruitment. Discussions with those who actually work in the industry can also be an excellent opportunity for the institute to review and/or upgrade its academic courses.

The importance of a major event like Pravaah will be accentuated by the three day Socio-Cultural event where activities of different genres shall take place.


The activities under the socio-cultural events shall be aimed at the manifestation as well as encouragement of talent across various institutions. A number of events will take place in collaboration with the prominent NGO's from across the country and fraction of the social events will be materialized in their association.
